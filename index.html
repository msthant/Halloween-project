What is Halloween?

Halloween is a holiday celebrated on every October 31.

The history of Halloween

@Valeria Sangiovanni / Dreamstime.com

Halloween began 2000 years ago when Celtic people celebrated on ancient festival called Samhain. Samhain is a celebration of the end of the summer harvest. It is celebrated from sunset on October 31 to sunset November 1st.

Who were first celebrated Halloween?

Getty_Images

Ancient Celts who lived in Northern Europe were the first people to celebrated Halloween. They believed that on October 31, the spirits of the dead roam or the ghosts came to our earth and would harm to us by many different ways. That’s why, people would leave food and drinks on their door steps for the ghosts and sprits. During Samhain, Celts dressed up in scary costumes and make noise in the streets to keep these sprits away.

Why do we call Halloween?

img from-Tehaleh

As Christianity grew influence in Europe, the Christian church turned Samhain into All Saints’ Day or All Hallows’ Day. All Saints’ Day is celebrated on November 1st and All Hallows’ Eve celebrated on October 31st. Later, its name changed to Halloween.

How do they celebrate Halloween?

JENNIFER_LAMBERT

Some families celebrate by having costume parties and playing special games like bobbing for apples and telling ghost stories. Sometimes children go “trick or treating”. It means knocking on doors in their trusted neighborhoods collecting candy. Many people also make lamps out of pumpkins.
